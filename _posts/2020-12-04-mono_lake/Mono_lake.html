Figure 1: Annual lake level measurements (feet above sea level) between 1850 and 2017 collected from the Mono Basin Clearinghouse. Lake levels that pose significance to nesting bird populations are noted with horizontal lines. At 6,377 ft above sea level, a land bridge emerges that allows predators access to crucial California Gull nesting habitat. Salinity reaches 120 g/L at 6,360 ft above sea level and 150 g/L at 6,350 feet above sea level, resulting in decreased food availability for Eared Grebes and California Gulls. Notable years are annotated on the graph (1941: LADWP began diverting water from Mono Lake’s tributary streams; 1978: Mono Lake Committee formed; 1983: California Supreme Court decision; 1994: State Water Board decision). Further information about these dates and more can be found on the Mono Lake website. Data: National Research Council (1987).

Citation:

National Research Council. 1987. The Mono Basin Ecosystem: Effects of Changing Lake Level. Washington, DC: The National Academies Press. https://doi.org/10.17226/1007.
